How asylum works — simplified

A general overview. Asylum rules vary by country — always check legal guidance for the country where you apply.

  1. Seek safe shelter — get to a safe location and contact local authorities or aid organizations.
  2. Register your claim — follow country-specific registration procedures and keep documentation.
  3. Find legal assistance — free legal clinics and NGOs can help with forms and interviews.
  4. Attend interviews — prepare your testimony and provide supporting documents.
  5. Appeal if necessary — legal representation can help if the claim is denied.

Documents that help

  • Identity documents (passport, national ID)
  • Birth and marriage certificates
  • Evidence of persecution (photos, messages, medical records)
  • Witness statements

Common questions

What if I don't have documents?

You can still apply — explain why documents are missing and provide supporting testimony or alternative evidence.

How long does the process take?

Processing times vary widely between countries; some decisions take months, others years. Stay in contact with your legal representative.
